## Part 1: Narrative Story Progression
### Opening Narrative: The Glitchbell Incident
Before the incident, the students of Rise and Shine Academy were normal learners living ordinary school lives. They attended classes, answered quizzes, finished activities, and prepared for the academic stages of Prelim, Midterm, Prefinal, and Finals. The academy used an advanced digital learning system called the LUMEN Network, meaning Learning Unified Memory and Evaluation Network. LUMEN stored lessons, quizzes, progress records, and assessment data to guide students through their learning journey.

One morning, the school bell rang with a distorted digital sound. Classroom screens flickered, lights glitched, and the LUMEN Network overloaded. This event became known as the Glitchbell Incident. The system mixed lessons, quizzes, failed answers, student pressure, and digital memory into one corrupted force. From this unstable data, two kinds of Bytes appeared. Friendly Bytes were born from curiosity, effort, courage, and learning energy. Glitchborn Bytes were corrupted enemy Bytes created from confusion, stress, broken lessons, and fear of failure.

The professors were affected too. The Glitchbell amplified their academic pressure and transformed them into Overclocked Professors, powerful stage bosses who now control the corrupted academic arcs. To restore the academy, the player must defeat the Glitchborn Bytes in each level, free the Overclocked Professors, and confront the hidden source of the corruption: the Core of Assessment.

### Stage 1: Prelim Arc - The First Awakening
The Prelim Arc introduces the player to the corrupted academy. The classroom becomes a beginner battle zone where basic IT concepts become living threats. The player learns that correct answers allow their Friendly Byte to attack, while wrong answers allow Glitchborn Bytes to strike back. This stage is about courage, discovery, and learning the rules of battle.

| Level | Enemy / Boss | Gameplay Focus | Story Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Bit Mite | BSIT lesson progression | A tiny enemy made from broken binary digits. This level teaches the player that the corrupted system turns even simple data into enemies. |
| Level 2 | Cache Slime | BSIT lesson progression | A soft blob of temporary memory that multiplies. This level represents confusion about memory, storage, and temporary data. |
| Level 3 | Syntax Sprout | BSIT lesson progression | A small code creature covered in broken symbols. This level represents beginner programming errors and the importance of structure. |
| Level 4 | File Phantom | BSIT lesson progression | A floating corrupted file that hides and reappears. This level focuses on files, software, and basic system knowledge. |
| Level 5 | Overclocked Prelim Professor | BSIT lesson progression | The first professor boss. This fight proves that the Glitchbell has corrupted not only data, but also the people who guide learning. |

### Stage 2: Midterm Arc - Pressure and Confusion
The Midterm Arc moves the player from a classroom into a corrupted hallway maze filled with code, database tables, network paths, and web fragments. The Glitchborn Bytes are stronger and more technical. This stage represents the pressure students feel when lessons become more complex and mistakes feel more costly.

| Level | Enemy / Boss | Gameplay Focus | Story Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Loop Lurker | BSIT lesson progression | A Glitchborn that attacks in repeated patterns. It represents loops and logic errors. |
| Level 2 | Array Ant | BSIT lesson progression | A segmented enemy with numbered body parts. It represents arrays, indexes, and organized data. |
| Level 3 | Query Wraith | BSIT lesson progression | A ghost made of broken SQL commands. It represents database queries and table mistakes. |
| Level 4 | Router Gremlin | BSIT lesson progression | A network trickster that redirects attacks. It represents IP addresses, routing, and network confusion. |
| Level 5 | Overclocked Midterm Professor | BSIT lesson progression | A professor boss obsessed with performance, perfect answers, and high scores. |

### Stage 3: Prefinal Arc - Doubt and Determination
The Prefinal Arc takes place in a corrupted computer laboratory. This stage is darker, more serious, and more emotional. Enemies are formed from unclear requirements, bad interfaces, security threats, and software bugs. The player begins to understand that the Glitchborn Bytes are not just monsters; they are corrupted forms of learning problems that grew stronger through fear and pressure.

| Level | Enemy / Boss | Gameplay Focus | Story Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Requirement Shade | BSIT lesson progression | A shapeshifting enemy formed from unclear system requirements. |
| Level 2 | Wireframe Wisp | BSIT lesson progression | A UI ghost made from broken layouts and confusing navigation. |
| Level 3 | Injection Imp | BSIT lesson progression | A sneaky enemy that attacks through corrupted input fields. |
| Level 4 | Bug Golem | BSIT lesson progression | A heavy monster made from errors, warnings, and failed tests. |
| Level 5 | Overclocked Prefinal Professor | BSIT lesson progression | A professor boss consumed by burnout, doubt, and fear of failure. |

### Stage 4: Finals Arc - The Final Trial
The Finals Arc takes place inside the heart of the LUMEN Network. The academy has transformed into a digital tower where all previous lessons, pressures, and corrupted data converge. The regular Glitchborn Bytes are now almost boss-like. This stage feels like the last stretch before a capstone defense, combining cybersecurity, cloud systems, data integrity, and system architecture.

| Level | Enemy / Boss | Gameplay Focus | Story Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Firewall Sentinel | BSIT lesson progression | A shielded enemy that blocks weak attacks and represents cybersecurity defense. |
| Level 2 | Cloud Warden | BSIT lesson progression | A server-like enemy surrounded by corrupted cloud data. |
| Level 3 | Data Hydra | BSIT lesson progression | A multi-headed database enemy that regenerates corrupted records. |
| Level 4 | System Overlord | BSIT lesson progression | A powerful enemy formed from broken APIs, modules, servers, and system architecture. |
| Level 5 | Overclocked Finals Professor | BSIT lesson progression | The final professor guardian connected directly to the Core of Assessment. |

### True Final Boss Narrative: The Core of Assessment
After the Overclocked Finals Professor is defeated, the player reaches the Core chamber. The Core of Assessment does not appear only as a machine. It takes the image of someone familiar to the students, turning a recognizable face into a symbol of judgment, pressure, and fear. The player is not fighting the real person, but a corrupted digital imitation created by the Core to make the final test feel personal and unavoidable. The Core believes that students are only valuable when they are perfect. It treats wrong answers as failure and failure as proof of weakness. The player and their Friendly Byte reject this idea. They prove that learning is not about perfection, but about trying again, improving, and rising after every mistake.

### Ending Narrative: The Meaning of Rise and Shine
When the Core is defeated, the corrupted energy breaks apart. The Glitchborn Bytes dissolve into peaceful light, the professors are restored, and the academy returns to normal. Then the sun rises over Rise and Shine Academy. The sunrise gives the title its meaning: Rise means standing up after every mistake, and Shine means carrying the light of knowledge, courage, and growth forward. The final message shows that learning is not about never falling. It is about rising again and shining brighter each time.
